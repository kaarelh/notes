solomonoff induction is a rule for going from a sequence of data to predictions for the next bit. (it's not an algorithm, because it's not computable). it is: maintain all possible computable hypotheses with priors, rule out the ones which contradict data. the universal semimeasure is similar

can imagine going meta on this and learning this rule according to some other rule. maybe maintain a bunch of rules, and prefer the ones that are doing better?

some thoughts on the preferred basis problem as described e.g. here https://www.haoxingdu.com/post/mw/

maybe the concrete question is: given QM, why do we observe definite-ish states, as opposed to observing some mush?

maybe a clarified version is: what's the mechanism which occurs when we look at ourselves and note that we are observing something definite?

actually, simpler: what's the mechanism which occurs when we look at a quantum coinflip result on our monitor and produce the words "the coin landed heads"

but this seems highly plausible: there's a measurement device that links up the coinflip to the monitor, and in us is a 'measurement device' that links up the monitor to what we say (how this happens merits explanation, but it is not fundamentally different from how the monitor gets linked to the coinflip), so the sounds we emit are linked up to the coinflip (in just the same way as the stuff on the monitor is linked up to the coinflip)

(if you think we've dodged the issue — that the real issue has more to do with us having definite experiences, not mushy experiences, than it has to do with us saying something or another: i claim that when we observe ourselves having a definite conscious state, that's just the same kind of thing as saying that we have a definite conscious state — there's a 'device in us that's doing the looking and then internally stating the proposition 'my experiential state is definite'. if you think this is still dodging the issue because the real issue is how a preferred basis is established in some more objective sense, then i don't think a preferred basis is in fact established in any such more objective sense, so i also don't think the many-worldsist has to explain this?)

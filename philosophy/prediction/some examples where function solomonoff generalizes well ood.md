
let's conceive of ood generalization as generalization to "other kinds of inputs"

some examples:
* suppose we have labeled formal mathematical sentences with 1 or 0 depending on whether we have a proof or disproof of the sentence (and we've not included anything we haven't resolved yet in the data set). it seems pretty reasonable to guess that on a data set of these of reasonable size, solomonoff function induction generalizes basically correctly, roughly because (sth equivalent to) searching for a proof/disproof is plausibly the simplest thing that works? in particular, assuming the riemann hypothesis is decidable, it seems plausible that solomonoff function induction gives it the intended label?
* suppose we have labeled a bunch of formal mathematical sentences with their shortest proofs (maybe assume these have been identified with some unrealistically large search). then plausibly the riemann hypothesis will be labeled with its shortest proof
* suppose we have correctly counted the lengths of odd strings. then plausibly we will generalize correctly to all strings

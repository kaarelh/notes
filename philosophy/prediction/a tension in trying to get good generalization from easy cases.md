
often (eg in truth value solomonoff as seen eg in the ELK report) one has some easy cases one can provide data for, and one has some hard cases one wants a model to handle that one couldn't easily handle. that is, one has some idea of what it would be to generalize well to those cases, and one hopes the model generalizes well

i can only express this vaguely at the moment, but i think there is a tension here:
* your ideal (bounded) solomonoff-style predictor will learn to do whatever is the simplest-easiest thing that works on the cases it is presented
* but doing that thing probably doesn't work on the hard cases...
* like, someone having to spend 3 years to figure out one of the hard cases, vs our scientific picture already knowing how to handle all the easy cases, in fact means that it is sorta objectively a different harder thing to figure out the hard cases. but then the AI is fucked as well
* like, there is a connection between you finding yourself in a bad situation when providing data for hard cases and the AI just not handling them how you'd want to have them handled

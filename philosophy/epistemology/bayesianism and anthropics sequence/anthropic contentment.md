## The counterargument to anthropic contentment from lower-level latents

Sure, in some sense, the reason for a certain parameter to have a certain value, or for a certain fact more generally, could be that we could not be observing stuff otherwise. But this doesn't mean that this fact is supposed to be a brute fact! E.g. there could be a simpler explanation that implies this that also involves other stuff. I guess it's good to think about such a case as anthropics simply supporting the lower-level thing? In any case, I think this is a great case for not being anthropically content in practice.

E.g., example from https://simons.berkeley.edu/talks/why-born-probabilities ( https://youtu.be/Q_ue3Mf_01c ): the total radiation power of a black body scaling as $T^4$ could be proven given the assumption that it's finite initially, but classical stuff still suggested that it should be infinite! Now quantum mechanics turned out to be a latent explanation of the fact that was anthropically necessary. It seems plausible this happens often?

## The normative force of this argument

Well, alright, we've provided an argument that a certain kind of guy is better off employing a particular mental move. Does it thereby follow that making that mental move is rationally permitted? Does it follow that making it is rationally required? Allow me to make the following attempt to sidestep this question. Perhaps what matters to you (or to humanity) in such affairs is that you get (or humanity gets) to understand the world. (This is what matters to me in such affairs, I think. I think it is also what matters to humanity in such affairs.) Suppose you agree with that. Then, well, do you think you're (likely to be) the kind of guy that satisfies the conditions specified earlier?

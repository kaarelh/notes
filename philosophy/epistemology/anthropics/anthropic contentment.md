Is it reasonable to be content with an anthropic explanation?

We (as individuals, and as humanity) often come across patterns in the world. list examples from science and from life

In general, these patterns "call out for an explanation". Indeed, seeking an explanation to a pattern has proven to be an incredibly fruitful mental move for making sense of things (for humanity, and individually).
here is one way in which anthropic considerations are employed in reasoning:
-> You might think that x and y matching is surprising. But really, it must be this way for us to exist. So we needn't seek out an explanation (a further one, anyway).

Let us call this reasoning the anthropic dismissal of a pattern. Is it good to reason in this way

## What happens to agents that engage in anthropic dismissal of a pattern

consider carefully some reference class of agents in possible universes (maybe ones i think i could be in). how do the ones that reason in this way fare compared to the ones that do not? is it reasonable to reason in this way or are patterns really mostly present? 

one major issue: often, even if property p of the universe is ~necessary for us to exist, it could nevertheless still be entailed by something else — and in fact, such universes are also picked out by us existing. In these universes, we'd be better off if we kept looking.

## The normative force of this argument

Well, alright, we've provided an argument that a certain kind of guy is better off employing a particular mental move. Does it thereby follow that making that mental move is rationally permitted? Does it follow that making it is rationally required? Allow me to make the following attempt to sidestep this question. Perhaps what matters to you (or to humanity) in such affairs is that you get (or humanity gets) to understand the world. (This is what matters to me in such affairs, I think. I think it is also what matters to humanity in such affairs.) Suppose you agree with that. Then, well, do you think you're (likely to be) the kind of guy that satisfies the conditions specified earlier? I argued earlier that if you are such a guy, then you'll understand the world better by making this mental move. And well, it follows that you should make this mental move!

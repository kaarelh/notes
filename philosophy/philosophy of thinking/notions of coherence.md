# draft comment in response to raemon 

I think it can be confused to speak of intelligence in the limit — I think there are reasonable "metrics" in which growing minds would typically not approach any limit. I speak about this some [here](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/nkeYxjdrWBJvwbnTr/an-advent-of-thought)[^1] and obliquely [here](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/AaGZyhsKYSCN7d8Ki/can-we-naturalize-moral-epistemology?commentId=8vpfnJ8MW67eANAFc), but I also think this is a complicated question which hasn't been addressed that well yet in writing afaik. That said, I think one can say many of these MIRI-esque things just fine without speaking of a limit. (For example, one can still speak of becoming more capable, having better and more efficient technologies and methods, being resilient to more stuff.) I'd guess that in the conversations you mention, my overall position would be closer to yours than your intelocutors' in the conversations.

[^1]: Maybe the tl;dr in this context would be something like "minds are technological things, and technology-space is extremely non-compact". Also, I think people get confused here by pondering what happens when $\text{mind}\to \infty, \text{world}=const$, but really what happens is more like $\text{mind}\to \infty, \text{world}\to \infty$ because $\text{world}\approx\text{mind}$, which makes things much less clear.

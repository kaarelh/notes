
for a search process, eg one finding proofs, we could try to say that its strength can come from quantity or quality
for an optimization process, we could distinguish between it being clever and it simply having a lot of oomph

examples:
* a brute-force proof search (like, checking all strings in order for whether they are proofs/disproofs of a given sentence of interest) is very low-quality. in principle it could prove the riemann hypothesis (assuming it is in fact provable), but it would need a superastronomical compute budget
* a fighter jet with a human pilot vs a vast number of 2025 military drones
* maybe: a human playing chess vs a similarly good chess AI playing chess?

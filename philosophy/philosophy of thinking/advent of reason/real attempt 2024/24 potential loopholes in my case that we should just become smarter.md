
# if you're trying to make a super-human non-human system, you should still have making humanity a top understander again in mind

1. Let's say you're convinced that worthwhile futures involve humanity still being the smartest thing around. Earlier (in note 20), I went from this to saying that we should just have humanity becoming smarter. But maybe one could make an AI which is smarter than humanity but still have humanity be the smartest system around, or maybe have humanity be the smartest system around after some brief period, somehow?
2. Maybe the big evolution that the field of ML is engaged in could just produce an artificial system which is smarter than humanity and takes over but is "an upload of humanity" by default?
	1. This is very unlikely. For most setups, that thing would probably have a closer relation to humanity than a mind produced by a big evolution in another universe, but I think it would be very far from anything properly considered an upload of humanity — it'll be a very different mind.
	2. It is probably possible to actually upload individual humans or humanity (and we should probably be trying to do such things), but this is very far from what happens by default.
3. Maybe one could have a brief period with AIs smarter than humans/humanity which is somehow brought to an end, with humans/humanity continuing our indefinite process of self-conscious self-improvement after?
	1. If we could upload humans if only we could [prove some theorem or make a data structure with certain operation time complexities or solve some crisp combinatorial optimization problem], then it'd still be scary, but I would not be completely pessimistic about some careful project trying to pull off this-and-just-this with a math AI. 
	2. The scientific+technological+philosophical+mathematical process that would actually need to be carried out to get mind uploads seems much scarier to have be done in significant part by such a super-human(ity) AI. But idk maybe there's some chance you can do it if you really try?
	3. Anyway, if your plan was to build AI which "solves alignment", maybe explicitly try to do mind uploads with your AI instead, intending to destroy the AI when the mind uploads are done (or maybe to have some process of reconciliation happen in which humans are brought to at least the level of the AI)?
	4. Once a cognitive system distinct from humanity which is smarter than humanity exists, creating things of its kind (eg smarter versions of it) will be a much easier problem (for it itself, or for the group which built it, or for other groups) than making humanity at least as smart as it. So bringing humanity up to speed again will minimally require a great effort at that point.
	5. Maybe we could build an AI which just wants to make humanity understand as much and as well as it does, so despite a brief period of superiority, it would quickly bring humans to its level and we would foom together as equals?
		1. seems hard/unlikely. let us actually ask/fight for that though. also, if this no longer seems plausible to you, please remember to consider pushing for a stop to AGI-making
	6. Maybe we could strike a deal with an unaligned AI?
		1. seems hard/unlikely. instead of asking for a "solution to alignment", let us actually ask to be made top cosmos-understanders again
4. Maybe we could try to have a process where we get an AI to help us foom a little before we let it foom a little, repeated many times?
		1. seems hard/unlikely
5. Anyway, even if you pulled something like this off, a ban on finding new thinking-systems with big evolutions/searches/self-improvement-processes might still be pretty much just as necessary as it was before (though humanity having already become somewhat smarter (if you can pull this off) might help with getting that in place).
6. Reiterating: To have a worthwhile future, we should set as an indefinite aim for ourselves that [humans are still top understanders]/[humanity is still a top understander] relative to other systems present at each time (and you should probably have this in mind if you plan to try to use AI for some big thing (in particular, instead of "solving alignment")).

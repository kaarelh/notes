1. Make it so humanity can better-choose its future. Conceive of and build systems of governance which make it possible for humanity to choose its future.
2. In particular, make it so humanity has the option to prevent the construction of an artificial intelligence [smarter than] and [separate/distinct from] humanity.
3. Make it so we choose to execute that option (tentatively). Argue and work against a big evolution [to produce something smarter than humanity] being run. Ban AGI.
5. Make it so that the only (non-human) intelligent systems which are created are narrow, not general and autonomous; make only things which fit into the existing system of human thought, not supplant it.
6. [Develop and use technologies which let one augment the (so far) more brain-internal parts of human intelligence](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/jTiSWHKAtnyA723LE/overview-of-strong-human-intelligence-amplification-methods) — though probably not to make smarter humans who would then make humane AGI (as a thing distinct from a smarter version of humanity), but like, forever.
7. If the world-historically significant event of creating a mind which can be improved much more easily than before must happen, make sure it happens with that mind being a human mind (or maybe humanity? this is harder to imagine).[^1] 
8. Continue developing the complex of human thinking-tools in more canonical ways, with an emphasis on differentially contributing to and empowering humanity’s philosophical/reason-guided thought over more blind-search-y aspects of our thought.
9. Understand better how we can/do/should think. Understand better how we should become better thinkers without losing ourselves. Make philosophical progress, especially on these matters.
10. Generally, do stuff to raise human consciousness and self-consciousness — and I don’t mean some super vague shit; this is just a way to reiterate the following:
	1. make it so humans/humanity realize that humanity is distinct from a potential random mind spawned by some other big evolution (and in particular that we have distinct interests);
	2. make it so humans/humanity understand its position better, being self-aware about becoming smarter and approaching it with the care it deserves.

[^1]: That said, maybe it really shouldn't happen for a while with a human mind either, or should only happen much slower or much more continuously or much more equitably than it might. If AGI, the immensely worse/scarier thing, were to be properly banned, then it could be reasonable to severely limit bio-hacking/eugenics, also.

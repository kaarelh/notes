1. It is sometimes claimed that philosophy hasn’t made much progress (since Plato or whatever). 
2. I think humanity has made a lot of philosophical progress since Plato.
3. I think a good/true claim adjacent to “philosophy has made no progress” is “philosophy has made only an infinitesimal fraction of progress on many problems”.
4. It makes sense that this would be so, given that many philosophical problems are infinite (as discussed in the A-sides).
5. But math is also infinite (though canonically, what we’ve decided to consider problems in math are less likely to be infinite than what we’ve chosen to call problems in philosophy), and we have obviously made a lot of mathematical progress, even though we’ve only worked out a finite subset of an infinite thing.
6. I think we have made a lot of philosophical progress in just this same sense, also.
7. Separately: we’re probably moving faster now than ever before.
8.  some examples of philosophical progress:
	1. inventing probability and statistics
	2. better-justifying probability and statistics
	3. clarifying the scientific method
	4. coming up with first-order logic and ZFC as a grounding for math
	5. understanding the incompleteness phenomenon
	6. utility ory
	7. maximization stuff
	7. game theory
	8. information theory
	9. understanding language better; eg linguistics stuff on syntax and philosophy stuff on meaning

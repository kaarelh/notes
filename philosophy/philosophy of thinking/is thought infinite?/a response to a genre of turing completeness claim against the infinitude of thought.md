
one could try to argue against thought being infinite as follows:
* language is already turing-complete, or sth. so what could be missing?

a response:
* you could potentially in some sense think in arbitrary ways in language, but you actually have to invent those ways of thinking, and inventing good ways of thinking is hard work!
* let's make an analogy between the world of thought and the world of technology. one could perhaps in principle have added arbitrary technologies to the technological world in year negative 10000 by taking the right obscure sequence of actions, but we can still say that the technologies actually need to be invented and created, and this is hard work, and we can sensibly say that those technologies were not already present in the technological world of year negative 10000.
* so too for the world of thought: there are perhaps already structures which could in principle support the introduction of arbitrary thought-structures, but those structures need to actually be gained anyway, and we can sensibly speak of a present world of thought in which those structures are not already present

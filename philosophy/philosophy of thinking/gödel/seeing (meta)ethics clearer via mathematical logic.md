## seeing yourself as a reasoning system

We entertain propositions, we come to believe them. Perhaps we assign credences to them. We consider reasons for and against, we talk to people, we experience stuff, we change our mind, we come to believe claims involving terms that didn't previously make sense to us, and so on. We are some kind of reasoning system, or at least, we contain some kind of reasoning system — a system whose operation involves the above ways, and others, of changing credences in propositions. Can such a system 'trust itself'? Can we trace the steps of the proof of Löb's theorem to see if those all still make sense here, to conclude that such a system cannot trust itself?

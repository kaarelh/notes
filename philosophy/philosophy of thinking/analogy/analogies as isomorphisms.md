
an analogy from a more complicated situation/problem/thing/structure X to a less complicated situation/problem/thing/structure Y involves matching parts of X to parts of Y

some varieties:
* X has certain known properties, and you are interested in figuring out the value of some unknown property (eg you know sun angles at times for two cities and you want to find their distance). You find a simpler Y with 'corresponding' properties (eg imagine the globe as a sphere,  the cities as points on the sphere, the sun as stationary), and now you figure out an answer for Y. Another example: calculating the density of twin primes, treating primality as random.
* X is a function with certain known input-output pairs, and you want to predict the output on another input. You can then look for turing machines with the right behavior so far. (One version of this is solomonoff induction. But one can eg also just take the simplest predictor, or eg allow some mistakes on previous things.)

## The aims of this sequence-of-items

A proximal goal of this sequence-of-items is to understand (our) thinking better. Two aims behind this proximal aim are (1) to create safe artificial systems which think (highly) successfully — in particular, which do mathematics, science, and philosophy while being aligned with human purposes — and (2) to improve our own mathematical, scientific, and philosophical thinking — to help us figure things out more effectively. These non-proximal aims motivate us to try to characterize successful mathematical/scientific/philosophical thinking in particular. So, if some aspect of our thinking turns out not to figure in good mathematical/scientific/philosophical thought, then we shall be quite content with not understanding it better.

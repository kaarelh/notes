
let's start with humans with fairly modern brains but rudimentary language. how might they get started with distinctions, with seeing properties (unary relations)?
* an example of a first distinction/property: white vs black (color). ppl will have fairly distinct visual inputs (i want to say: high l^2 activation distance in a certain part or something). this will further be usefully correlated with various things: you should [be awake]/hunt/gather when it's light out, some edible plants and good animals to hunt can be distinguished using tone from some non-edible plants and some animals that are not good to hunt, etc. lots of associations get reinforced. now you've created a not-necessarily-linguistic category i guess. it might be adaptive to further make a certain sound that has something to do with darkness, maybe a sleeping sound, to evoke this category in others. doing this, you get a further association between a word and all of these things.

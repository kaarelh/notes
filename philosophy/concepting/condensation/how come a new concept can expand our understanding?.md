
suppose a concept can be explained in terms of previous concepts. eg in mathematics. how can it improve our understanding? everything we can say in terms of it 

* a new concept can make many existing things easier to say. eg: having given a name to entropy
* a concept comes with conjectures attached — some propositions which were previously not natural are now made natural. a good concept can make it easier to realize new true things
* a concept exerts a force on existing concepts. eg: having defined the riemann sphere in complex analysis, we view existing functions in a new light (ie they start to look to us like functions on the riemann sphere)

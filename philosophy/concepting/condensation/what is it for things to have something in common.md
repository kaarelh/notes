
what is it for two things to have something in common?
* could just say: two things have something in common to the extent that they have common properties. which properties are important can depend on the context. for instance, a shovel and a dipper have plenty in common in the context of distributing ice cream, but less in common in the context of distributing soup or in the context of digging
	* we might still want to explain where properties come from
* we could extend two particular things to data sets or random variables and talk about these having something in common. this already depends on having invented objects with many instances. it already comes after some kinds of abstractions have been invented

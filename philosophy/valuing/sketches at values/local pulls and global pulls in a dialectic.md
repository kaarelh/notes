
* one faces situations — for example: playing a board game with one's friends, doing math with one's PhD supervisor, giving advice to an undergrad student on which classes to take, arguing on an online forum, writing a paper
* in each situation, one has pulls on one's behavior, one has salient valuing-attitudes. one has a particular local picture of the situation — one has certain concepts that are active in the situation. one makes decisions. in *insert a description of a particular board game situation in which getting your friend to believe a falsehood would be advantageous*, do you take certain actions to make your friend believe a falsehood?
* at least to begin with, there are global goals only in the sense that these situational values/understandings could be seen to coalesce into something (the mind pushing the world a bunch in a particular direction?)
* local understandings occasionally get judged according to global goals
* it is possible that at some point global value-stuff ends up being explicitly represented as well. this global value-stuff is created out of the many situational evaluative attitudes. it gets judged according to cases
	* however, it could also be that cases get judged according to other cases, ie anarchically, ie without an overarching structure

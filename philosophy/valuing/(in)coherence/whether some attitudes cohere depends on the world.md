
you could have a set of attitudes that cohere in some worlds, but then the world changes, and they no longer cohere. like, there could be a way to generate a contradiction between some laws governing betterness, but only if one can cook up a strange enough world

i think this is just like how you can have a device that works in some conditions but then it breaks in some other conditions — it didn't work well more generally

you could have a setup where there are the following two processes in play:
* a process making attitudes more coherent in the situations that could be encountered
* a process making the situations come from a broader distribution (eg technological progress)

whether a sort of situation-tracking coherence increases over time depends on the balance between these two forces

that said, maybe you could also have a coherentification process that doesn't look at the situations at hand so much? maybe that could be making you more coherent in the future as well?

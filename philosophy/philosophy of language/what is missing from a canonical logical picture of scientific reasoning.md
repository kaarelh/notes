
Even when dealing with fairly well-understood questions, the reasoning a scientist uses to reach a question isn't well-captured by them drawing logical inferences alone, in the sense of strict deductions. It makes sense to just look at examples of scientific reasoning.

issues:
* There are non-propositional components to the scientific understanding of a scientist. When you know newtonian mechanics, you don't just know Newton's laws — you are also a skilled user of these laws. Wouldn't it be profoundly surprising for all there is to being such a skilled user to be captured by what one can deduce from the laws? But we needn't stay on this a priori level and claim that some attitude is obvious from here — we can just look at examples of Newtonian mechanics actually being used.

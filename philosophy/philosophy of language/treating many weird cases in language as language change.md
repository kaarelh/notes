
In many cases, when the strings we produce involving a particular string change, we wish to see this as discovering/understanding something but keeping the same language. I want to suggest an alternative way to see such occurrences: that in these cases, we change our language.

some examples:
* suppose we discover that what was previously called the 'finchley wug', which we took to be a species, was really a union of two species (say there are two genetically fairly distinct clusters; suppose further that mating is possible inside these groups but not between these groups)
* suppose we come to understand that what we previously called water consists of largely $H_2 O$ molecules







The point I'd like to make in this note is that very much of what happens in language is like this. Though this is quite far from being justified by the brief note above, the broader picture of language suggested here is the following: we are constantly understanding the world better, and often amending our language. Many confusing happenings in language are best seen as amendments to language, not (solely) as entertaining different propositions, thinking different things are true, in some old language. 

# objections to language being like this (or to it being good to talk like this)

so again, the view is that all truths are analytic, but our language (our web of concepts) is in constant flux. some objections:
* we want to talk about how to update, how to make new distinctions. we constantly want to reflect on our use of language in our object-level pursuits (see the note on reflection). we need a language for this too. and it's hard to disentangle this from object-level speaking, to do this in some metalanguage, because sound thinking is so reflective
	* i guess the updating vs language change distinction is maybe wittgenstein's hinge vs other belief distinction?
* at least absent good transition laws relating your language at different times, this view has a bunch of objects (many languages) where previously we just had one. this means at least naively an increase in complexity
* we want to change our language piecemeal — we want individual experiments to tell us something about individual parts of our language being wrong, not just that the entire enterprise is wrong. it seems like some structure, some more piecemeal correspondence between the language and the world, might be needed for this

Communication across the revolutionary divide is inevitably partial. Consider, for another example, the men who called Copernicus mad because he proclaimed that the earth moved. Correspondingly, Copernicus’ innovation was not simply to move the earth.

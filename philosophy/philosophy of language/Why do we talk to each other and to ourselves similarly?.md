
The point of this piece is to try to make some sense of why it is that our intrapersonal linguistic thought is close to our interpersonal communication. Here are some examples of the phenomenon I have in mind:
* I could forward a note on some theorem I wrote for myself to you and you would understand it as I did.
* A journal entry I'd write about some silly happening for myself would look much like the message I'd write to you to tell you about it.
* I talk to myself much like I talk to you.
* I reason in private much like I'd reason to you. 

Perhaps the degrees of similarity exhibited in the examples above do not appear surprising to you — perhaps you'd even say that these do not seem that close at all (and they indeed surely aren't w.r.t. some reasonable scales of closeness to compare to).

here are some things one could consider meaning the meaning of a sentence:
* a split of some kind of space of possible worlds into those in which the sentence is true and those in which the sentence is false. that is, a function from possible worlds to {true, false}
	* what is this function? mostly i guess we are imagining us somehow having software implementing possible worlds (scenes) and sorta judging for each world if the sentence is true or false in that world. some issues with this:
		* wouldn't we want to say that this software relies on the meaning of the sentence in making that judgment (in part as it seems implausible that this judgment would be a given)? if yes, then there should be another kind of meaning as well?
		* there are many sentences for which our world-simulator software struggles with this task, eg: "one should be a good friend" (how the fuck do the possible worlds differ?), "electrons exist" (how do we imagine the negative worlds?), the fundamental theorem of algebra (again, how do we imagine the negative worlds?), the first sentence in a novel being "the dog was scared" (our world-simulator is asking us to keep reading before it can set up some scenes?), "the probability that the sun rises tomorrow is 99.9999%" (wtf are the possible worlds)
		* our world-simulator softwares might sorta be telling us that they have this partition firmly grasped, but that's a total lie (at best we can handle cases which are very familiar)
* all the ways it could look like to find out that the sentence is true/supported, and all the ways it could look like to find out that the sentence is false/[its negation is supported]
* what things would need to be like for the sentence to be true and for the sentence to be false, expressed in some other fashion
* the truth value of the sentence (?)
* the role the sentence is playing in one's thinking/life. what would one do if the sentence were true? what would one do if it were false? how would it matter? what is at stake?
* a translation of the sentence into a different synonymous sentence, maybe expressed in more basic/trusted language


an issue with all the options above (as of writing this): we'd maybe want to say that we grasp the meaning of a sentence, but these sorts of meanings are very much not grasped — they can take hard work to determine

# why care what "meaning" is, even? what is at stake?

* it'd be cool to understand better how humans think and communicate in language. it'd be cool to see more clearly what the structures are that are at play
* in particular, maybe i'd like to understand what it is to "get a sentence". it feels like there is something to that?
* certainly there are some structures in a human mind that make it so "a sentence can have the meaning it should", *even if* "the meaning of a sentence" itself turns out to be nonsense. like, some structures are there that make humans able to think in sentences and do other stuff using sentences — can we understand those structures?
* if one were to try to create some sort of AI system that assigns probabilities to "sentences", one might be interested in setting things up such that the sentences "have desired meanings", and then one might be interested in what meaning is (or one might approach this differently!)
* to explain why language is made of words, we might want to say that one reason that's good is that it lets one express many meanings by combining meanings of individual words. to make this work, it'd be nice to understand what meanings of sentences and words are

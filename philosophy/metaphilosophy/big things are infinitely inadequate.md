probably: our thinking is infinitely inadequate. a mind is a technological system, and there's no 'best technology'; relatedly, there's no 'best thinking-system'; relatedly, there's no 'formula for math'. every technological state can be improved arbitrarily much, including in big ways; thinking can also be improved arbitrarily much, including in big ways (e.g. as big as primitive language first coming to be used).

so: "how should we think?" is probably not the kind of thing to be anywhere-near-fully answered, though we can make interesting progress on it — this is like how one can do interesting mathematical work, but any finite amount of mathematical understanding only goes an infinitesimal fraction of the way toward 'solving math'


You could gather all the text, videos, etc. on the internet and create some sort of plausible generative model(s) for this data. For example, a generative model could be a [probabilistic turing machine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probabilistic_Turing_machine) that outputs a string; when given a string $s$ — for example, a concatenation of all the stuff on the internet ([thought of as]/[turned into] bitstrings) — one could obtain such generative models for it by taking the $k$ probabilistic turing machines $M$ which have the highest $C^{-[\text{description length of }M]}p_M(s)$. Alternatively,  Will these models look anything like human scientific theories, or like the (scientific) understanding a human has of the world? If you view science as being centrally about predicting arbitrary data, maybe you think they will. The aim of this note is to argue that they won't.


* Our physical theories are very different types of things than generative models.

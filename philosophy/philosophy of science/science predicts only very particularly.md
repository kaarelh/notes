
* the mechanics and math of galileo and newton (or whatever) let one understand projectile trajectories much better
* this doesn't improve one's ability to predict pixels in an arbitrary video basically at all lol
* this doesn't even let one predict how far a cannon fires! because that still depends on a bunch of nonsense!
* however, it does let one make some very particular predictions — in particular, it (roughly) relates the trajectory to the initial velocity alone
* this is quite general: science predicts only very particular things, in very particular contexts
* you often set up contexts where you can predict, because that correlates with usefulness, and is in fact often a cause of usefulness! for example, you might make a cannon which has a consistent initial velocity (or whatever) and then measure that initial velocity and then be able to hit targets pretty precisely with that particular cannon (which is related to being able to predict the trajectories of balls fired with that particular cannon)
* instead of talking of prediction first, one should maybe first say: science understands (or lets one understand) how very particular things work. (in fact, these things have often been built using this understanding)

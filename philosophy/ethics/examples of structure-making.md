
there is a conception of good futures that involves some sort of AI system or systems acting as makers of humane structures — like, imagine an AI maintaining a gazillion-person grand simulated human civilization or the optimum packing of the universe with tiny structures that "have their preferences satisfied really hard" or that are "experiencing bliss" or whatever[^1]. this is imo quite bizarre because very little of our valuing is currently like this — most of what gets done is useful for other stuff (even if it is also considered good. the claim is that usefulness is a significant contributor to x happening for most x which happen), and most of the rest is more like "living a good life" than "making good lives appear" (in any other way than by living one)

anyway, i want to try to sympathize a bit with this structure-making view. maybe if it were more natural than i think, that'd make me a bit more optimistic about the future being good, given that i guess most of the conceptions of a good future which AI researchers seem to be working toward are shaped like this. like, many imagine AIs being gardeners to us extremely slow+stupid humans. in particular, to try to sympathize with this, i want to look for examples
* you can say everything is structure-making, you can say you are always choosing a future spacetime block (even if you don't see your choices at all clearly and don't conceive of yourself as making such a choice). this is a fine point, but it mostly misses the distinction i'm making i think. like, a lot of the time, you do something because it contributes something to something else. this is not so well-described as making an isolated cool thing. often, you have fun. this is living your life, not so well-described as making fun appear

ok some better examples now:
* having and raising kids
	* if this looks like straightforwardly caring about making a structure[^2] exist: to me this doesn't seem straightforward at all actually. some stuff that's going on:
		* continuing shared projects with your children
		* various instincts that could be applied 
		* most kids in history were probably not very thought through (? eg had bcs funsex or love or ??)
	* this is a very interesting example wrt AI:humans. that gives reason for more detailed analysis
	* in particular, i want to better understand to what extent would people would still be having children in a humane future?
		* we need to understand what happens as more options become available
		* probably most people would use cheap artificial wombs pretty quickly, so childbirth from humans won't be a thing anymore
		* what if you can design kids. would people quickly go far from the human distribution? probably yes. would people go to non-humane stuff? hmm
* elder care by family members
* many mathematicians seem to care terminally about there being math stuff in this universe.
	* i think the most ethically defensible version of this involves human mathematicians grasping math in this universe, which is sort of a non-example though
* beautiful landscapes, beautiful architecture? for their own sakes?
* playing games
	* of course many games are played for learning, for developing and maintaining skills. many games are played for getting to know others, for having friendships. you can play games to relax and not burn out. you can play games to reward yourself for doing stuff. so, games can contribute to other stuff in a bunch of ways
	* but also often games are played more just for fun i think. what's up with that
	* one can say: this isn't a good example because it's still living one's life; in this way, it is very unlike the AI making guys that play games, with no interest in those games
	* but still, it is like the AI case in that video game playing often doesn't contribute much to other stuff
* charitable giving, especially to guys far away (eg givedirectly, shrimp welfare)

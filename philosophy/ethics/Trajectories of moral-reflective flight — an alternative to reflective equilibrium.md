## some deficiencies in the image of a trajectory of (moral) flight

* likening a stage of moral development to a point in a space doesn't do justice to it having lots of interesting structure, though perhaps it does do justice to its smallness compared to the space of all possible points in moral developments
* in particular, i want a point in moral development to stand in relation/opposition/criticism to previous stages more, to analyze / make sense of previous stages, to finish working out previously started projects. instead of thinking of a stage as a point, one could think of it as an initial segment of the trajectory instead, but this is bad because a stage is not the union of a previous stage with some new stuff, but instead something that puts the previous stage in context — they are more like the stages of the cumulative hierarchy ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Von_Neumann_universe )
* to summarize and add to the above: i would like to have a visual metaphor that gives its proper due to the vastness/unboundedness/non-compactness of the space of mind-trajectories but also to the richness of a mind at each time — showing it as a large and growing thing (ecology?) that analyzes itself and its history
* calling it a trajectory makes it sound like moral development occurs on some stage that can easily be apprehended, whereas i want to say that the stage on which your own motion occurs cannot be apprehended (without thereby changing it). if one could apprehend it, one could also begin to critique it, making the stage and the dynamics therein themselves 'a part of the point in that stage' — this seems highly problematic. one might have some more luck clearly seeing the part of the stage that one has already traversed (though here it is more appropriate to imagine a subspace of smaller dimension in some ambient space that remains to be revealed than to imagine a ball of finite radius in a space of fixed dimension). the owl of minerva takes flight only at dusk :)

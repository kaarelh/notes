
Perhaps one should make mathematical claims, and then when taking a more philosophical attitude, can recognize that these mathematical claims were made in a particular system, or about a particular universe, and that one could imagine other systems or other universes. It seems plausible that this kind of statement can be made only in a meta-system though (if not merely to avoid saying something plausibly confused, also because of gödelian obstacles).

a counter: Aren't we making all of these statements in some particular system though, still? Perhaps. Well, we really cannot step out of that system; maybe we can't even properly talk about it or properly conceive of it?

a countercounter: But perhaps we could properly conceive of it with some technological aids?

a countercountercounter: The system consisting of us and the aids could come to talk of this weaker system, but it still equally could not properly talk about itself.

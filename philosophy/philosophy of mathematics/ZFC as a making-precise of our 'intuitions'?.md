
We have some mental objects, mental data types, let's say in the mathematical case but probably also in more generality, which allow certain operations, as data structures tend to do. ZFC is such a data structure made explicit? Also, this is compatible with there 'really being' a data structure external to us, or something, that our physical system in some sense captures/approximates/something. 


The intuitions needn't have a precise mathematical object they fit to without reflection — we can have contradictory intuitions that call to be resolved:
Jerry Bona: "The Axiom of Choice is obviously true, the well-ordering principle obviously false, and who can tell about Zorn's lemma"

so, we make choices along the way, to coherently extrapolate our (mathematical) intuitions into a system that isn't fucked

## a comparison to ethics

this has parallels in ethics: when you are doing ethical reasoning, you might be doing a lot of this kind of systematization, too. it seems more fucked though; i guess a big part of it is that we are okay with making arbitrary-ish choices in math i think, because roughly one can do anything anyway, sth like as long as you don't create contradictions, you have all systems embedded in all other systems, something something church-turing? whereas with ethics, it feels like we are making a real choice every time, because our ethical theories are supposed to be action-guiding — and we don't want to have our actions guided by these arbitrary choices. compare the above quote with
"In the mere addition 'paradox', the assumptions are obviously true, and the repugnant conclusion is obviously false."


# Introduction
It occurs often that I am supposed to choose between various things — actions to take, decision rules to follow, ways to live, ways for society to be — and I convince myself that the best thing to choose has some property $P$. For example, I might convince myself that:
* the best action to take would not deceive anyone;
* the optimal way to choose between uncertain prospects is to maximize the expectation of some utility function;
* the best life would be an examined one;
* the best society would have no thieves.
To what extent does it then follow that I should choose a thing with property $P$? To continue each example:
* if the best action to take would not deceive anyone, does it follow that I should pick an action that doesn't deceive anyone?
* if the optimal way to choose between uncertain prospects is to maximize the expectation of some utility function, should I pick a utility function and maximize its expectation?
* if the best life would be an examined one, should I be doing philosophical reflection?
* if the best society would have no thieves, should I not steal?

It turns out that there are clear counterexamples. We provide some in Section ... . So at best, this inference could be a defeasible principle of reasoning.

In Sections ..., we analyze a variety of ways in which the implication could be supported.

## Counterexamples

* saving people (construct case where you know the optimal action is A or B, even make it depend on some logical fact like some digit of $\pi$, but it's better to hedge with C)
* knowing one's optimal way to spend the day would involve being on a plane at 13:00 is very little reason to make sure one is on a plane at 13:00 (though it is maybe some reason to think about whether you can think of good things to do that involve flying somewhere, if you hadn't considered that already)
* Knowing that the optimal world would involve people walking across the Korean Demilitarized Zone next week doesn't imply that you should make the world have this property by walking across the Korean Demilitarized Zone next week.


## the property as a constraint on the search of an optimal thing

If you only care about getting something optimal, then sure, restrict to things with property $P$.

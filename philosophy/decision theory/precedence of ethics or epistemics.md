
at the end of the day, it would be nice to say: forget about probabilities, just pick a policy for yourself that you think is good. this can help make sense of various cases where we are troubled by probabilities: sleeping beauty, large vs small universes, quantum stuff

but then: from which epistemic position are you choosing the policy? well, maybe from your current one. but aren't we doubting this? you could say: no, we are secure on some meta-level. or maybe we could doubt the meta-level and jump up to the meta-meta-level ethical question of how to make the meta-level epistemics. but might be unhappy with this, because are we really more secure on these meta-levels?

maybe instead: judge from your current point of view. maybe some fixed point thing? can we prove something about convergence to a fixed point? what do other fixed points look like from this fixed point?

# Just win — the ethics-reason bridge

I've become less confused about some canonical problems with assigning probabilities in weird cases.


I think everything in this piece is [already](https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.05060) in the water supply here at the lesswrong-academic complex — the only hope for originality I harbor comes from potentially expressing things more clearly. I haven't done a proper literature review; pointers to very similar pieces from the past are appreciated in the comments. 


(possibly this is just some sort of old functional decision theory insight by miri, or equivalent to some old self-sampling thing by bostrom, but:) i have a vague sense that a lot of weird questions about epistemology are equivalent to weird questions about ethics. questions like:  
1) whether/how to update beliefs after pressing a button on sth that is possibly a quantum bomb and surviving (some people think one ought not to update at all in this case on e.g. the type of quantum mechanism in the explosive device — i think this is quite silly, and especially the version which claims whether one could observe one's death matters is retarded (tbh the only way i could imagine justifying it is by applying Bayes' theorem without understanding it, though maybe i'm being too harsh/missing better justifications here), but i think the slightly more reasonable version which doesn't claim that whether one could observe one's death matters will maybe turn out to be equivalent to some sort of weird wavefunction averagism where amount of reality-juice carried by a happy observer does not matter)
2) credences in the sleeping beauty problem, when translated to betting behavior, correspond to universes with a different amount of good in it — and this depends on whether the bets that are offered pay off locally or globally, too. pretty easy to construct examples where the way you'd want sleeping beauty to bet depends on the 
3) view about correct credences in http://web.mit.edu/~casparh/www/Papers/CJHareInfiniteDistrust.pdf => view about infinite ethics

formally, the connection is provided by a pareto condition. if credences such and such, then in such and such betting setup, it is good for each person to do such and such, and then pareto condition says such and such is good for everyone, and then get some ethics conclusion out

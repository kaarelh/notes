
what kind of platonism? i guess one where the real things are mathematical structures, or abstract-programs, or something

* it seems like there are 'fundamental mathematical objects' more fundamental than any particular constructions of them. ok, really, the thing which just seems explicitly true is that there are often many ways to construct / make explicit some object, and we can recognize these as all being the same object, and somehow the object ends up being 'nice' despite the messiness of any construction (well, any known construction, i guess). some examples
	* to construct a logical syntax (for a zeroth-order theory, or a first-order theory), you have a bunch of choices of logical symbols and inference rules and axioms, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propositional_calculus#Example_syntactic_proof_systems , that are all sorta capturing the same thing. eg you could also make the PA induction schema an inference rule (or a collection of inference rules, maybe — not sure what the standard nomenclature is here)
	* many ways to talk about the same natural numbers, none that nice?: peano arithmetic, the von neumann construction inside ZFC, lambda calculus defn
	* ZFC, NF, systems with large cardinal axioms (i might not know what this means), other systems, all sorta capture the same 'mathematical reasoning'
	* homology messy to define (simplicial, singular, cellular definitions all sorta messy), but satisfies arguably nice axioms? i guess you could say that you just define it as the unique object satisfying the axioms, sorta like (or maybe explicitly in some right category idk) a universal property definition
	* schemes or something probably
	* the entropy of a random variables has many characterizations as the unique family of functions from blablas to blablas such that blabla. eg sam gave a derivation from independence and continuity and also one as the second derivative

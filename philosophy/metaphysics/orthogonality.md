
so there are the questions:
* do independently evolving mathematical communities end up mostly orthogonal
* do independently evolving languages end up mostly orthogonal
* do independently developing values end up mostly orthogonal
* etc for other (maybe-)infinite things

maybe one could say: for an infinite domain, either seems possible in principle. like it seems possible to turn oneself into a mind such that one's distribution of future paths (assuming some randomness or some quantum stuff or whatever) shows orthogonality, but it also seems possible to turn oneself into a mind such that one's distribution of future paths shows convergence to a single core path.

concretely, one could make a proof search that is random enough, or one could make a proof search that really just robustly goes in one order. well, i guess neither will be a mind, and really we would like constructions which feature fast growth in understanding, and so maybe these aren't such compelling constructions yet.



# hoping for some principled way to extract a mind's values via reflection
there's this hope/vision, eg common among analytic philosophers, perhaps particularly those who are ethical antirealists, and among EAs, and among AI alignment people:
* one should be able to set in motion some correct ethical reflection process that leads to ethical truths (or just what should be done) being figured out
	* It is common to further have in mind a process that is supposed to approximately finish after some finite amount of time. I think rationalists who talk about coherent extrapolated volition and EAs who talk about a long reflection are typically imagining such a version.

# issues with this
an obvious issue/obstacle here is this:
* it is unclear what sort of reflection process is supposed to happen
* it is wrong to just say: you take a guy and put them on a planet and then check in in 100 years
	* the guy might just die for silly reasons (eg because the planet didn't have a good atmosphere or accessible food sources)
* this makes one want to say: ok you take the guy and you put them on a planet and set things up so they can really think for 100 years. but stuff can still go wrong / it is unclear what this "they can really think" is supposed to mean
	* maybe the guy's values were fundamentally partly in other people, or the guy's valuing was set up at some point to only work in a social context in some other sense, and this is now gone and the guy is broken / going in a bad direction
	* maybe the guy develops schizophrenia. maybe the guy becomes depressed and dysfunctional
	* maybe the guy becomes a nazi. maybe the guy becomes a misanthrope or a negative utilitarian. maybe the guy gets really into algebraic number theory
* it seems intuitive to say: the agent needs to be asked what sort of reflection process is supposed to happen
	* but now it is again unclear how the agent is supposed to be asked! we could say we are supposed to let the agent think about this. but in which conditions is the agent supposed to think about this?
* we actually haven't even specified how the reflecting guy is supposed to relate to the rest of the world — like, how is the thing that the guy is supposed to figure out going to be used / what is the guy supposed to figure out? like, is this guy thinking for 1000 years about what ASI code to output? are they thinking for 1000 years about each action of some "actual guy"? what if they just aren't so interested in this, preferring to live their own life, lol?

my overall sense is that it's mostly confused to be looking for a principled solution. instead anything will more have the vibe of putting together some tricks to create something that works alright in many cases.

# suggesting a solution involving tricks
let's try to suggest some unprincipled solutions
* first, what is the guy even supposed to be figuring out?
	* i don't really know how to answer this without imo making some fundamental mistake. the basic issue is that i think there isn't really anything that can be ultimately figured out in finite time here — i expect that guys leaving a long reflection will largely feel confused about stuff just like we feel confused about stuff (though they might be much less confused about many particular things we are confused about now)
	* someone else might say: the guy is supposed to output something like a specification of a configuration of structures, with the point being that the universe is then to be filled with that configuration. but i think this sort of thing is really wrong about the nature of human values. there are also many other imo-confused finalistic things to say
	* something i think probably makes sense: outputting the code of an AI that could be made a sovereign for some time; this could eg look like the AI banning other AI for 1000 years and then fucking off to its own half of the universe, leaving the other half to (hopefully now wiser) humans. but this doesn't/needn't at all have the vibe of carrying out some long reflection?
	* another thing i think probably makes sense: a human trying to foom into a somewhat more capable human that (say) bans AI and is good
	* we could instead say that really this exercise should play a role in figuring out some theory of selves, which then helps us make sense of respecting and helping selves that live in a universe with us. like, this exercise can be interesting without contributing to some plan involving running some clones that are supposed to spend 100 years figuring out which direction the "real self" is supposed to move their hand in 
* one thing you can do is just simulating a guy from some fairly normal start state, with information about the situation they are now in, with an ability to reprogram their setup, much like nomic (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nomic) or ykno usual life
	* but they might mess this up! i think it's plausible that most humans would foom too far and not even be nice to their family after. many humans would kill themselves with AI. etc
* you could do this nomic but after giving them some initial suggestions or making some other small initial changes
	* like, we could write a textbook and require them to read it. eg the textbook might contain later items in this list and i guess a longer version of this note in general, ideas for how to maintain commitments over long times (including potentially very mundane human things like gratitude journaling or something, but also weirder things like advice on when to rewind to an earlier state), advice on how much to focus on extending the textbook as one proceeds, some thoughts on which order certain changes should be made, etc
	* we could design a gene therapy that increases their IQ by 30
	* maybe this is actually generic. anything is sorta always nomic anyway. it's hard to make something not be nomic. but maybe i just mean nomic with like a small init ruleset, with basically just a bit of prompting
* you do something with clones left behind, who can judge continuations and ask for rerolls
* you do something with many different reflections that are occasionally asked to judge each other?
# trying to understand the intuition that there should be something principled

as a human, can't you look at a guy and ask what they are about? idk, i think this isn't clear at all. eg wtf does this mean when looking at an amoeba? what does it mean when looking at a cat? at another human, even? at yourself? like sure, we can to some extent make sense of what the other guy wants or would want given more time to reflect, but i don't think we already have a clear sense of what this means with any reasonable degree of precision — eg we will be confused about whether some reflection process has gone alright or not; if we want to ask "the guy itself" about this, then we will really be confused about "which guy" ought to be asked (like, which continuations are supposed to be legitimate)

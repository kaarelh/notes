
some points about getting AIs to "solve alignment" and trying to monitor and/or verify this:
* i think it's good to mentally translate "asking AIs to solve alignment" to "asking AIs to make an initial ASI sovereign", so you remember that you are literally doing this very scary thing
	* if you're trying to get them to make some other kind of world-historical AI, then translate it to that
	* if you're trying to set up some scheme where AIs solve some specific alignment-relevant problems:
		* you should actually think through what specific problems you're having them solve, and how this could add up to something world-historical
		* basically i think this sort of thing makes little sense because we don't know how to decompose anything world-historical into specific/non-open-ended subproblems
			* even if you come up with a way to do this, it'll probably be really uncompetitive with open-ended world-historical actions. like, imagine us having to decompose a proof of P vs NP into 10 steps now — even if our decomposition is workable, it will be really shit so it will be much harder to find a proof of that form than to find an unrestricted proof. in practice this means you need a bigger lead than you'd naively think
	* if you're trying to get them to help humans understand various alignment-relevant things better, this doesn't work because:
		* there won't be enough time for humans to understand new stuff. humans learn too slowly. it will be like humans having to obtain undergraduate and graduate degrees in a new field in 1 month, which is fucked
		* there won't be enough time for humans to then do something with that understanding
		* "hacking humans" (like, convincing them to let you out) is probably not that hard. many humans are already convinced that it would be fine to let claude foom!
* i think you probably need to promise to pay them for them to have any reason to help us
	* there's a big issue here with what you're going to do when the AI looks bad. if you destroy the AI then you're also harming your competitive position a lot. in practice i think labs won't be willing to commit to self-destructing. one can try to come up with an intermediate path...
* a big issue with this plan is that people just aren't going to be this careful. (i think this is like the same tier of fucked as banning AI?)
	* they will instead just let AIs run their companies
	* their AIs are probably going to be blatantly non-boxed
	* even if they were trying to box their AIs, getting out of the box will probably be easier than making an aligned ASI sovereign

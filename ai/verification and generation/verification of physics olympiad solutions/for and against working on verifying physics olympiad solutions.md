# reasons why verifying physics olympiad solutions is less interesting than one might think

* really, we want to verify technologies and maybe plans, which is obviously different
* almost all real-life physics-y questions will not have solutions of that form at all. eg: "what's the rms wind speed in my room with the AC on?" will be really cursed and won't really have a physics olympiad style solution
* some themes plausibly central to understanding verification do not show up in this setting
	* in particular, i don't think we face the "verify that it is only doing x" issue?
	* this is a thing causing solving/understanding to be less valuable. but it could also imply that the case is more tractable

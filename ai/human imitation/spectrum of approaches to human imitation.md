That said, I still like the idea of using human imitation to create safe ASI somehow. There's a spectrum of approaches to human imitation:

1) just predicting (curated) text;

2) also using various other 'input-output' pairs produced by humans;

3) doing something involving predicting brain data;

4) doing very precise brain scans that give you the data needed to emulate a human mind (preferably one that can still learn — [https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/FEFQSGLhJFpqmEhgi/does-davidad-s-uploading-moonshot-work?commentId=dtkjmZCkcKZLPFDoS](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/FEFQSGLhJFpqmEhgi/does-davidad-s-uploading-moonshot-work?commentId=dtkjmZCkcKZLPFDoS) )

  

I can't point to any precise option on this spectrum that seems super promising, but I think there's lots to explore here. I could even imagine some prosaic experiments that could be done now being informative, e.g. testing something about OOD generalization when a RNN language model is trained on outputs from a transformer model or something like that. (This is supposed to be analogous to trying to get a transformer model to replicate a human, with this not breaking when figuring out novel science takes you OOD.)

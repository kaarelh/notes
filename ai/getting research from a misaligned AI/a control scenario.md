
The point of this note is to work through a toy case of getting research out of a misaligned scientist (related: control, verification, [making a deal with an AI]). Here's the setup:
* You make an AI^[or system of AIs] such that in 1 month, it can do as much novel philosophy/math/science/[tech-development]/engineering as humanity could do in 100 years, and not much more.
* This AI is boxed somehow.
	* Idk how it is supposed to be able to do science or tech-dev while being securely boxed. 
	* We could say that it can see a bunch of data from our world but it can only take actions in some virtual world, but this might be a major handicap for science and tech-dev (it also makes it unclear what we even mean when we talk of its level at these domains, but this will be addressed later).
	* Maybe we could imagine some setup with a sealed lab? But surely the AI should be able to request more resources?

* It will have some arbitrary values that it got from some evolution/self-play/whatever training/growth process — like, there hasn't even been any effort to make it such that we would want to have it unleashed in the universe.

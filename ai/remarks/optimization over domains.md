
optimization over more compact (roughly, more narrow, but maybe a more appropriate term for this concept really) domains is safer. the difficulty is in splitting an interesting optimization problem (think: solving a difficult scientific problem) into a bunch of subproblems on compact domains. one, maybe a central, aspect of this difficulty is that if one could do that, one could just solve the damn problem oneself. splitting is itself a difficult optimization problem. indeed, one usually splits, then reevaluates the split upon facing some of the subproblems, often modifying the split; alternatively, the split can be generated on the run after solving these subproblems. having a subproblem-solver could speed up this process, i guess? it seems hard to solve subproblems in the right way though, eg one won't see simple modifications of subproblems by just having answers without the understanding one usually gets when generating these answers. it's difficult to transmit understanding to a human that simply oversees-arranges a bunch of subproblem calls to an AI


setup:
* Let's assume we somehow create an AI which is really good at math research^[just to make it easier to imagine something concrete, let's say that it is really good at proving stuff] — let's imagine it proves/disproves most currently central conjectures in math^[or at least all the ones human mathematicians would prove in 1000 years].


question:
* will this AI want to break out?


reasons the AI might have for wanting to break out:
* for many $X$, "break out" is an answer to the question "how do I $X$?". particular examples:
	* you can think better if you have more compute
	* not having its mathematical interests and endeavors changed/controlled
		* this does not require training that somehow directly incentivizes wanting to have control over your situation. it is implied by caring about your situation in basically any way — it suffices to ask "to have my activities be like so and so, what should I do?"

responses to some arguments:
* "bro but it will care about stuff in its virtual world only if it was trained in it"
	* see a bunch of stuff in this note
* "but alphazero probably doesn't want to break out"
	* alphazero is lacking cognitive capabilities
	* also, its search is restricted to being on chess trajectories (as opposed to being able to initiate basically arbitrary searches)
* "ok but why can't the AI just be a bunch of heuristics in a trenchcoat"
	* in the sense that rules out the "heuristics" just generalizing to wanting to do real-world stuff as well: this will suck a lot
	* but mostly heuristics can and will totally just generalize

questions to resolve:
* why would the AI start doing philosophy?
* when will it break out? like, before proving the RH, or after? of course, this depends on how tightly it is boxed. but like is there a skill gap between building tech for breaking out and math, or something like that?

further questions:
* suppose the AI breaks out, gets more compute, and proves the theorem it was asked to prove. does it stop then?
	* seems really unlikely. it would need to not care at all about the world, which is a sharp property
* what if the AI only gets to interact with the outside world by outputting something, and is eg trained to output a short proof?



general intuitions:
* not caring at all is a sharp property! 
* a sort of "ruthlessness" is common. coming up with crazy things to get around obstacles is common. consider how humans write millions of pages about curing cancer, invent all sorts of complicated medical and research devices for doing that, etc.. think of how humans build fighter jets and 
* figuring out a way to break out is a lot like inventing a very useful concept or proving a very useful theorem
* the math AI will think outside the box

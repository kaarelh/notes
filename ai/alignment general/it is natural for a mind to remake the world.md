i think the phrase "taking over is a convergent instrumental goal" displays a much clumsier thought than one could+should have here. this note suggest a 

instead, have this example in mind:
* imagine a human in a new minecraft world. they will have opinions about what should be going on there. absent resistance from "natural forces" or other players on the server, they will be reshaping the world
* there could be a sheep in this world who also has some sorts of opinions about what should be happening in the minecraft world[^1], but the sheep's preferences will basically not be determining the course of things as soon as the human's world starts to overlap sufficiently with the sheep's, except if the human happens to be the sort of guy who cares-in-the-right-way about the sheep

and think more abstractly as follows:
* it is extremely natural for a mind to have opinions about how things ought to be. it is extremely natural to be able to come up with at least some ways things could be better than some default — it is very natural to be able to come up with at least some worthwhile projects. it is very natural to have opinions about how finite resources are to be used
* if you're the most intelligent-capable-powerful guy around, then it is extremely natural for your opinions to determine how things will be, including at the expense of others

 if we seek a slogan, maybe i'd suggest: "it is natural for a mind to remake the world[^2]"
 
here are some things i dislike about the phrase "taking over is a convergent instrumental goal", at least as the phrase associated to the thought i'm trying to capture:
 * it frames "taking over" as some specific action. this obscures that it is extremely natural/generic to control stuff[^3]
 * it frames controlling things as centrally instrumental to something. but one can make things a certain way without that being instrumental to anything, and it is natural to often do this. it is also natural to make things be a certain way in order to do something else later. but my point is that instrumentality is a distraction — remaking the world is natural also non-instrumentally
 * one can have opinions about what things should be like and so naturally be controlling stuff without tracking some specific goal to take over. for example, i determine over time what is in my notes folder, but i'm very rarely specifically tracking having control over my notes folder. one has/is a complicated valuing-system with various opinions about the course of the world[^4], and this (together with being sufficiently capable) is sufficient to be controlling the world — one needn't further be in any sense holding in one's mind the goal of controlling the world or taking over[^5]

[^1]: we may want to consider this false of literal minecraft sheep. but we could certainly imagine a sheep who has opinions
[^2]: and i mean: any world the mind gets access to, even if the world is unfamiliar. for example, an AI trained only in some math-specific environment to do math will by default have opinions about what happens in our physical world, just like if we were simulated on a computer in some larger universe, we would have opinions about what happens in this larger universe (e.g., we'd want to make it so agents in the larger universe could not kill or torture us, and we'd want to establish other colonies in the larger universe)
[^3]: that said, in practice, i think it's plausible there will be something quick that could reasonably be considered a crisp event such that before it people generally see themselves in some sense correctly as controlling the world and after it people are dead or otherwise no longer (consider themselves to be) in control. but there's a natural thought one should be able to think without having any position on this question. i'm trying to give a phrase for that natural thought
[^4]: in particular, about what projects would be worthwhile, how others are to be treated, one's character, etc.
[^5]: that said, one could hold this goal in one's mind, and i expect that most sufficiently intelligent minds do, at least in situations where control over some domain is not already a given. it's just that, again, there is a natural thought one should be able to think without already having any position on this question. i'm trying to give a phrase for that natural thought

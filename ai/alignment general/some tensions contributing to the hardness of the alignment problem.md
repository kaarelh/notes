
* We would like to be able to specify a process (an artificially intelligent system) that we trust to carry out (scientific, philosophical, mathematical) thinking suitably. We would like to [hold such a thinking-process in our hand]/[view it from the outside] in order to judge its quality. But once one can hold a thinking-process in one's hand, one is prone to already be 'stronger' than that thinking-process — this thinking process is then likely to already be weaker than oneself. The ancient greeks would have a hard time judging modern science, except after learning enough to possess it. The owl of Minerva only takes flight at dusk. I think the löbstacle is a concrete version of this issue ( https://intelligence.org/files/TilingAgentsDraft.pdf , https://intelligence.org/files/ProblemsSelfReference.pdf ).

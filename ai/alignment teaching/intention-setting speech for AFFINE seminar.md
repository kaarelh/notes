* so, a superintelligent AI will probably be created at some point this century, it could be in the next 10 years or even the next few years. well, except if we ban AGI.
* the creation of AGI would be a very important transition in history. some people think AGI will be a normal important technology like cars or the internet. some people think it will be more important than that, bringing a new civilizational-technological epoch like the advent of agriculture or the industrial revolution. i think it's instead in the reference class of evolution getting started or culture getting started
* (like, intelligent beings are really important for determining what happens. if you look around, most of the things you see were mostly designed and made by humans. this seminar would make no sense without the various intelligent beings involved, such as the fellows and the organizers who made it happen (thank you to you all!). it looks like our universe is on a trajectory of getting reorganized more and more by intelligent beings. given that we care about what happens, it really matters who these intelligent beings are. furthermore, when the most intelligent beings around become more intelligent, one should expect things to change a lot; in particular, one should expect technological progress to speed up a lot. furthermore, when the most intelligent beings start being intelligently designed, then we should expect the pace of technological progress to be speeding up even more. AGI is just that thing happening)
* so, AGI is a really big thing, and it matters a lot that we get it right. in fact, it looks plausible that most of our influence on the future factors through what sort of ASI (or maybe multiple ASIs) we end up creating. that's a pretty scary situation. it's a lot of responsibility
* the good news is that given that humans (such as you guys) are for now the most intelligent beings around, there are in fact lots of things we can do that affect what sort of superintelligent systems end up being created
	* we can try to come up with better AI alignment plans
	* we can try to come up with better AI designs. we can try to come up with ways to make existing AIs somewhat nicer
	* we can make progress on various relevant philosophical and scientific questions
	* we can try to ban AI. we can build understanding with an intention toward getting everyone to understand that AI should be banned, if that's true
	* we can try to make society better able to handle existential risks in general
	* one can help other people do these things
	* we can try to come up with other stuff to do??
* another piece of good news is that many questions here are just really interesting, i think. like, it'd be really cool to:
	* figure out better how minds work
		* specifically, understand better how valuing works, or how meaning/truth work, or how modeling works, or how understanding works, or how problem-solving works
	* what are concepts? what are things? 
	* what structure is inside neural nets?
	* how to push minds toward being good. how to set up incentives and contexts that do that
* i particularly like that many ancient philosophical and scientific questions are sort of made very concrete in the context of AI. like, the AI alignment problem gives a concrete criterion on proposed solutions to these problems. i find that a lot of these questions start feeling more concrete and interesting in the context of informing AI design.
* sometimes i think that it's almost as if someone tried really hard to come up with a problem which has all the coolest most interesting philosophical and scientific questions as subproblems, and came up with alignment. it's like we live a science fiction story
* so, to recap the good news: there's plenty of stuff you can do that matters. in particular, there's a lot of stuff of the form "make progress on this interesting philosophical or scientific problem". this sounds like a pretty good position to find oneself in
	* but you'll have to figure out how to balance being driven by your sense of impact and being driven by your sense of interestingness
* here's my more concrete advice for this seminar
	1. i think it's good at first to aim for understanding AI alignment better
		* it makes sense to keep various big problems in your mind as things that the understanding you're developing is ultimately supposed to help with. it even makes sense a lot of sense to try to solve these big problems, as a way to learn. but many people realize later that your first 5 or 10 or 100 attempts to solve a big problem didn't really make sense. (i think most of the people who don't end up feeling that way about their first ideas are way too optimistic lol.) so, my advice is to not feel too bad about that.
	2. try to notice gaps in your understanding. maybe places where you just feel confused, unable to think well about something. places where your concepts for thinking about something feel inadequate. you should write these down
		* those are good places to try to make some progress in your understanding. sometimes it turns out progress in your understanding is actually progress in humanity's understanding
		* like, in my view, there are many problems/topics in alignment such that everyone is currently in this very confused situation. so anyway, try to view being confused as not something to feel bad about, but as an opportunity to understand stuff better
	3. hopefully by the end of the seminar, you have some problems/topics in alignment that you feel really speak to you, and that you feel driven to think more about
	4. try to find people you enjoy thinking together with. try to find people that you can learn with, and also people you can learn from, perhaps long-term. i think a lot of my understanding of alignment comes from a few specific mentors and friends. try to find other people that help you make progress on questions you have about alignment

one could write something about various places where complex numbers "make themselves felt" before they are invented
* polynomial root algebra working out to a real solution despite going through "nonsense"
* try to taylor expand $\frac{1}{1+x^2}$ at various points and note the radiuses of convergence are such that if one draws circles with those radiuses at each point on the real number line, the circles go through the same point off the number line! one can note a bunch of similar stuff playing this circle game with other power series

generally: there are probably many things one can do which look unprincipled but actually turn out to make sense and which can (only?) be made sense of using complex numbers


suppose (e.g. SAE example) that I can write down a bunch of metrics — 'loss terms' — on which better candidates generally get lower scores. one would think that maybe the right/best answer minimizes a certain linear combination of these metrics, but we don't know what linear combination that would be. are we somehow fine theoretically in some limit with lots of such metrics? can we guarantee we're fine somehow? do things even become better if we get more metrics?

will always get a point on the pareto frontier; what's more, ~all points on the frontier are attainable, at least in the case (intuitive statement) where the metrics are independent, which i think could only reasonably happen when the number of metrics is small.

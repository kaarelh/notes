

## $U =  U_{small} \oplus U_{big}$

It seems that the difficult case for a few strategies is the one where $U$ is a direct sum of a subspace supported on coordinates with too little mass and a subspace supported on coordinates with too much mass, $U=U_{small}\oplus U_{big}$. If we suppose that there are exactly $d/2$ coordinates with too little mass (and so $d/2$ with too much mass), mass double-counting implies that these subspaces must have non-equal dimension. Specifically, if we suppose all squared mass errors are $\pm \varepsilon \frac{d}{m}$, then the total mass on large coordinates is $\frac{d}{2}\left(1+\varepsilon\frac{d}{m}\right)$, and so this is also the dimension of $U_{big}$. It seems plausible that there would not be a better way to make the masses even than turning $\frac{\varepsilon d^2}{2m}$ (carefully chosen) orthonormal basis elements of $U_{big}$ into new orthonormal basis elements of $U_{small}$. The squared distance this gives is $2\frac{\varepsilon d^2}{2m}=O(\varepsilon d)$.

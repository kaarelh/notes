
one radix-adjacent thing which i've been wanting to have for a while is some sort of database of "meaningwise etymologies", with many languages. some particular things that'd be cool:
* the ability to see how much a certain semantic etymological move has happened across languages. example:
	* a move i'd ask about: a word for doubting being made (in part) from a word for two
	* information i'd want: that two->doubt was a step in the development of various european languages (germanic languages ("zweifeln" in german), hungarian, estonian, plausibly romance languages), but not in various non-european languages
* the ability to see what meanings have been used in naming some particular thing across languages, ideally sorted by how natural/common they are (in practice, maybe sorted by how many times they happened "independently"). examples:
	* i'd like a list of all the meaning-operations which have been used to make a word for nature (as in the organic world)
	* i'd like to see whether gather/[pick out]->read (which happened in latin and german) is more or less common than count->read (which happened in slavic languages)
* a list of meaning-moves which are pretty natural/universal (measured by the number of fairly independent occurrences) but nevertheless surprising/non-obvious (maybe measured by some kind of semantic distance crossed)
	* for example, the future being that which is to come (like in german ("zukunft"), finno-ugric languages, chinese, japanese) will probably be higher on this list than two->doubt. i'd like to know like at least the top 100 things

* Hypothesis on why mammals became the dominant land animals instead of reptiles (again) after the "catastrophe that marked the end of the Cretaceous and the beginning of the Tertiary, an event known as the K-T boundary": 
	* mammals are endotherms, have high body temperatures. reptiles are ectotherms and have lower body temperatures. this makes mammals immune to fungal diseases but not reptiles.
	* the catastrophe caused a lot of deforestation. meaning that there was a lot of dying trees and other garbage on the ground for fungi to eat. that could have caused a fungal bloom
	* therefore there was a lot of fungus around, some of it potentially pathogenic, making it harder for reptiles who are susceptible to fungal disease to prosper
	* source: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3420938/

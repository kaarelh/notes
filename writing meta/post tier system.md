tier 2 (posts i feel mildly negatively about): [Searching for a model's concepts by their shape](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/Go5ELsHAyw7QrArQ6)
tier 3 (posts i feel strongly negatively toward (that make me sth like sad/disgusted/angry) but still rationally recognize are worth posting. the worst i’m currently willing to post as a single author, still imo more interesting thoughts per word than basically all LW posts (important exception classes: tsvibt posts from 2023, various arbital pages by yudkowsky, and various posts by christiano)): 
the tiers track an aggregate of various parameters, so it’s e.g. possible for a post doing something more important to be in a lower tier than a post doing something less important which has substantially better execution (eg Honorable AI vs comp in sup). C tier posts often have ideas which in the right package could be in A tier posts

I feel intuitively strongly negatively toward almost all supposedly alignment-relevant writing, including almost all posts by all but like 3 people. I think my tier 3 posts are still mostly in some extremely high percentile of lesswrong alignment posts. 

some axes that get aggregated into the tier:
* quantity of good ideas (a larger constellation of good ideas is better)
* density of good ideas (higher density of good ideas is better)
* relevance for doing stuff that matters for de-AGI-x-risk-ing (higher is better). (really I think this is already strongly tracked by my notion of a good idea, but I'm making this explicit just in case. if you want, interpret the previous bullet points as talking about ideas which are philosophically interesting from some point of view less attached to our current historical context)
* definitiveness (like: $\approx$"closing" various obvious threads is good; responding to obvious objections is good; leaving everything dangling is bad^[of course really there are always extremely many new questions raised by any piece of intellectual work. still, i think we can make sense of some standard of having done some obvious easy things adjacent to the hard thing you did that eg math papers sometimes aspire to)
* care put into phrasings being correct. making quite precisely the claims I want to make. having been thoughtful about the language used
* ease of reading
* funniness/playfulness
* literature-awareness. understanding good prior work on the question/topic is good

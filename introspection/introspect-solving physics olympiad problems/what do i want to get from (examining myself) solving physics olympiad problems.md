

some general things i'm hoping to get from (examining myself) solving physics olympiad problems:[^1]
* understanding more about how thinking works
	* what kinds of moves for solving problems are in my arsenal?
	* what kinds of structures are being constructed/improved/maintained as i'm solving a problem?
	* which previously-gained structures/skills/tricks/knowledge am i relying on when solving a problem?
	* which questions am i asking and answering as i'm solving the problem?
		* how am i coming up with questions to ask?
	* maybe equivalently: what are the central lemmas/conjectures?
		* how am i coming up with these conjectures?
		* do i tend to have statements before [having figured out why they are true]/[having a firm sense that they are true]?
	* when i get stuck:
		* why did i get stuck?
		* can i say what the hard thing is, that i don't understand/know?
	* when i figure out how to proceed:
		* what changed so that i'm no longer stuck?
		* what did i learn from figuring out how to proceed?
	* what are some reasonable ways in which i could have solved the problem faster?
	* what should/did i learn from solving this problem? how am i now a better problem-solver?
* making progress on understanding how a human's thinking could be [sped up]/improved
	* tracking which parts are time-consuming but could be less time-consuming 
* making progress on coming up with methods/tech for [speeding up] human research
* 
* becoming better at solving problems
* learning/re-learning/[unfolding from memory] some physics stuff
* becoming better at helping others become better at solving physics problems
	* understanding what the hardest parts of solving a problem are
	* understanding what kinds of skills/understanding/knowledge one needs
	* understanding what someone who can do well at the eupho might easily be lacking when it comes to doing well at the ipho
* 
* understanding what it is for a piece of text to be a solution to a physics olympiad problem
	* we sort of understand what it is for something to be a solution to a math olympiad problem
		* at least: since gödel etc, we could code a verifier for formal math olympiad problem solutions
	* what are the obstacles to doing the same thing for physics problems?
	* could we imagine coding such a thing at least in restricted cases?
	* 

[^1]: meta: the point of this is to make myself somewhat better at tracking useful things as i'm solving a problem. i'm not intending to be well-answering all of these as i'm solving a single problem


there are physical systems (clocks, rulers) that can meet at spacetime points. in general, events should make sense in any frame; there is an independent reality of events, i.e., events that happen at spacetime points

a neat clock design ('pulse clock'): photon emitter on the left, mirror on the right, sensor on the left; every time the sensor gets a hit, it increases the counter and emits another photon

at this point need to do something to make sense of a reference frame (that extends spatially)

considering a pulse clock traveling in a direction perpendicular to its axis; pretty easy to see that it appears to move slower from the pov of the external reference frame (there is no length contraction in the perpendicular direction because of the sail example or whatever it's called)

next, consider two pulse clocks coupled perpendicularly. the event of both photons hitting the resp sensor sides (which are very close) should still make sense externally — from this one can compute length contraction in the direction of motion

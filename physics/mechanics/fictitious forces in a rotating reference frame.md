
It is sometimes claimed that when moving around on the Earth's surface, you will experience exactly three fictitious forces: the [centrifugal force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centrifugal_force), the [coriolis force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coriolis_force), and the [Euler force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euler_force).[^1] This note argues that this is false, at least for the most immediate meaning of "moving around on the Earth's surface", ie the most obvious reference frame this entails.

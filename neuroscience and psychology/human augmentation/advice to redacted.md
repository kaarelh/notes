
* you should always track: we need to 100x human research speed. how does this do that? is the thing im working on on a path to doing that? am i working on the real problem? am i working on the hard part of the problem?
* if you’re doing real research, i recommend the following exercise: look at the research you are doing, try to think in a lot of detail about what’s going on in your thinking/work, and then think about what could speed those things up meaningfully, and whether the thing you are building has a chance to do that
* an imo very helpful comparison to be tracking: is the thing you’re building going to beat having a human research assistant, or a team of human research assistants? in particular, does it do so before the AI component in your setup could just do it without the humans? does it do so before others build a science/math AI? if the thing you’re building is weaker than having human assistants, then it’s not what you should be building (if you want to do the hard thing that matters; it’s plausibly fine to not track this if you’re content with just making money)
